The Gisèle Pelicot rape trial has been in limbo for years, with the government now demanding that the trial is moved to February.

The accused, Abdelkadera Boujir, was convicted of raping and sexually molesting a 12-year-old girl at a party in the city of Lesbos last June.

But the government has refused to provide details about the case and has said it is not interested in moving the trial to February.

The government said the trial would be moved to February because it is "a matter for the authorities to decide".

A court in Brussels has ordered the trial to be moved to March, but has been unable to provide details.
