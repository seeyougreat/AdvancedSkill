The number of people hospitalized nationwide for respiratory ailments and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease rose to 5.2 million last year from 3.8 million in 2011, according to the latest Health Insurance Marketplace Survey. That's up from 2.2 million in 2011.

In 2012, there were 3.6 million Americans under age 65, compared with 1.2 million in 2011.

The health insurance marketplaces have become increasingly popular, which could make them more effective at controlling the costs of care.

In 2012, nearly 2 million people were uninsured, according to the Health Insurance Marketplace Survey, which was conducted with more than 2,800 U.S. adults, mainly in the health insurance marketplaces within the health insurance marketplaces, and by the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services (CMS).

The survey of 1,664 adults found that there were 1.2 million people uninsured during the month of May, up from 1.3 million in 2011.

The survey also found that the number of people covered by the Affordable Care Act, the law that repealed the Affordable Care Act, has increased by 3 percent since 2011.
