Trump and Pence met with Russian Foreign Minister Sergey Lavrov Saturday and discussed Russian efforts to undermine the U.S. government, according to people briefed on the meeting.

The meeting was the second in the past week.

The administration began to target Russia's oil companies after the U.S. election, and the administration has recently been criticized for blocking Russia's state-owned oil company Rosneft.

Trump on Saturday also fired FBI Director James Comey, who investigated possible Russian meddling in the 2016 U.S. presidential election.

A White House official said Saturday that the president was considering new sanctions against Russia. The decision is expected to be made in early January.

Trump previously said he would impose further sanctions against Russia over the U.S. election, after the FBI declined to publicly release more information about its Russian investigation into former President Barack Obama's campaign.

Trump has repeatedly rejected calls to impose further sanctions. But he has promised to do so if he wins the White House.

Putin has also denied the allegations of Russian meddling in the U.S. election.

Trump said Saturday he was prepared to "totally understand" Russia's use of cyberattacks against U.S. political organizations.
